Cox's Bazar

Culture & Heritage

Over a thousand years of Rakhine civilisation, Buddhist temple architecture, and a living coast culture unlike anywhere in Bangladesh.

Since the 9th Century

The Rakhine People

The Rakhine — known here as Rakhain — are a Buddhist community who settled this coast over twelve hundred years ago. Their wooden stilt houses, hand-loom weaving workshops, and distinctive Khyang temples exist a short ride from the main beach, largely unchanged in character for generations.

Their language, food, and festivals — Bishu, Prabarna Purnima, Buddha Purnima — belong to a cultural world completely separate from Bengali mainstream life. To visit a Rakhain village is to step sideways in time.

Buddhism on the Coast

Pagodas, hilltops & silence

Aggameda Khyang

One of the oldest Buddhist monasteries in Cox's Bazar — gilded Buddha figures, carved teak interiors, and a courtyard of deep stillness in the middle of the town.

Ramu Buddhist Temples

Twelve kilometres inland, the village of Ramu holds a cluster of Rakhine wooden pagodas on forested hilltops — among the most atmospheric religious sites in Bangladesh.

Adinath Temple, Moheshkhali

A Hindu Shiva temple atop a hill on Moheshkhali Island — reached by boat and a climb through jungle. Sacred to both Hindu and Buddhist communities.

Living Culture

Markets & the Shore Economy

Tekpara

The Burmese Market

400 shops of Burmese textiles, pickles, cosmetics, seashells, handwoven lungis, and goods you will not find anywhere else in Bangladesh. A sensory overload in the best possible way.

Nazirartek

Shutki Palli — The Dry Fish Village

The largest dried fish production hub in Bangladesh. 40,000 families. 85% of the country's dried fish supply. Walk through and the air itself becomes an experience — salt, sea, and sun-dried fish drying on endless bamboo racks.

The Harbour

The Fishing Fleet

Hundreds of brightly painted wooden trawlers leave before dawn and return mid-morning. The fishing harbour at Nazirartek is one of the most photogenic working ports in South Asia — raw, colourful, and completely alive.

When Culture Comes Alive

Events & Festivals Calendar

🌙

Eid-ul-Fitr & Eid-ul-Adha

Dates shift yearly on the Islamic lunar calendar. Major domestic travel rush — book well ahead.

🇧🇩

Independence & Victory Day

March 26th and December 16th. National holidays with busy long weekends at the beach.

🎉

Pohela Boishakh

April 14th. Bengali New Year celebrations bring colour and crowds to the town.

💧

Bishu Festival

Mid-April. The Rakhine New Year water festival — among the most vivid local celebrations.

🏮

Prabarna Purnima

October/November, lunar calendar. The Buddhist "Festival of Lights," with lantern releases at Rakhine temples.

☸️

Buddha Purnima

April/May, lunar calendar. Observance of Buddha's birthday at Buddhist temples across the district.

🪔

Durga Puja

September/October, lunar calendar. The Hindu community's largest festival — elaborate pandals go up across the district, closing with an immersion ceremony held on the beach itself.

🎄

Christmas — Boro Din

December 25th. A national public holiday in Bangladesh, observed by the local Christian community.

Lunar-calendar festival dates shift every year — confirm the current year's dates before planning around them.

The People

A coast shaped by many hands

Bengali fisherfolk, Rakhine weavers, Rohingya communities, Hindu temple-keepers, and traders from across Myanmar — Cox's Bazar is not one culture but many, sharing one extraordinary stretch of coast.
