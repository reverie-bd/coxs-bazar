Cox's Bazar

Food & Dining

Where the catch is hours old, the kitchen is open-air, and every meal tastes of the sea.

SEAFOOD

Fresh from the Bay

The catch defines the kitchen

The fishing fleet leaves before dawn and returns by mid-morning. By noon, what was in the Bay of Bengal is on a grill. Cox's Bazar has no fine-dining pretension — it has something rarer: absolute freshness and cooking that has been perfected over generations.

Lobster, red snapper, tuna, squid, crab — grilled over charcoal on the beach or in the simple open kitchens of Kolatoli. Order by weight. Point at what you want. Eat it looking at the sea.

Most kitchens in Cox's Bazar serve halal cuisine as standard. Vegetarian dishes — bhorta platters, vegetable curries, dal, and rice — sit comfortably alongside the seafood, and restaurants are generally happy to adjust a dish on request.

What to Order

The essential dishes

Grilled Lobster

Ordered by weight, grilled whole over charcoal, served with mustard and lime. The definitive Cox's Bazar meal.

Seafood

Bhuna Hilsa

The national fish of Bangladesh — hilsa cooked slowly in mustard oil with turmeric and chilli. Rich, complex, irreplaceable.

Bengali Classic

Kala Bhuna

Chittagong's signature beef curry — slow-cooked until almost black, intensely spiced, served with plain rice. A non-seafood essential.

Chittagong Speciality

Bhorta Platter

A selection of mashed vegetables and dried fish — shutki bhorta is the most local — eaten with mustard oil and steamed rice. The everyday meal of the coast.

Local Staple

Stuffed Shrimp

Large prawns stuffed with spiced coconut and herbs, steamed or grilled — a Rakhine-influenced preparation found in Cox's Bazar and nowhere else.

Rakhine Influence

Mohinga-style Noodles

Burmese fish noodle soup influence — found in Rakhine restaurants near Tekpara. A bowl that tells the story of the coast's Burmese connections.

Burmese Heritage

Where to Eat

Restaurants worth finding

01

Beachfront Open-Air Dining

Beachfront, open-air, fusion cooking — the most atmospheric kind of setting in Cox's Bazar. Look for fresh seafood, wood-fired dishes, and a menu that changes with the day's catch.

BeachfrontFusionSeafood
02

Local Seafood, No Frills

Straightforward local seafood cooking — very fresh fish, reasonable prices, no fuss. The kind of place regulars come back to again and again.

LocalSeafoodValue
03

Home-Style Bengali Cooking

Bengali home cooking at its best — Kala Bhuna, Hilsa, Bhorta platters. Look for the kind of restaurant with no menu on the wall, because the regulars already know what's good.

BengaliHomestyleLocal
04

Live-Catch Dining

Choose your fish live from the tank and watch it cooked in front of you — a style of dining found at a handful of spots in Kolatoli. The freshness is guaranteed; it doesn't get more direct than this.
