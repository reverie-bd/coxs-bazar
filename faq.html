Quick Answers

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Cox's Bazar safe for tourists?

Yes — the core beach and town areas (Laboni, Kolatoli, Sugandha, Inani) are calm, well-trodden, and used to tourists. If your plans extend further south toward Teknaf, it's worth checking current local conditions before you go.

How many days do I need?

Three days covers the beach, Marine Drive, and one nearby destination like Himchari or a Rakhine village. Five days lets you add Saint Martin's or Moheshkhali without rushing.

Is alcohol available?

Alcohol is served by a number of licensed hotels and restaurants.

Do I need a local guide?

Not for the main beach and town — it's easy to navigate independently. A guide is useful for the Himchari trail or arranging island boat trips, and most hotels can arrange one.

Which beach area is best for swimming?

Laboni and Kolatoli have flagged, supervised swimming zones. The Bay of Bengal has strong currents elsewhere, so stick to flagged areas and avoid swimming alone at low tide.

Is it good for families or solo travellers?

Yes to both — Kolatoli/Sugandha is the most convenient base for families, and the area is generally comfortable for solo travellers, including women.

Can I drink the tap water?

No — stick to bottled water, which is cheap and widely available, including at most hotels.

What should I wear?

Many visitors choose breathable, light clothing for the heat, wearing swimwear on the beach itself and a cover-up when moving into town or markets.

Is it okay to take photos of local people?

Most visitors ask before photographing people directly, especially in villages, markets, or at religious sites.

Is tipping expected?

Tipping isn't required, though many visitors leave a small amount for hotel staff, guides, and drivers.

Can I negotiate prices?

Negotiating a price beforehand is common practice for CNG rides, boat hire, and souvenirs.

Can I use foreign currency like USD or EUR?

Bangladeshi Taka is the currency accepted directly at most businesses. Foreign currency such as USD or EUR can be exchanged at the airport, banks, or hotels.

Should I get a local SIM or use roaming?

Local SIM cards (Grameenphone, Robi) are inexpensive and offer the best coverage along the coast. International roaming is also an option for shorter stays.

Should I travel independently or book a package tour?

Both approaches are common. Independent travel suits the main beach and town areas well, while package tours offer a convenient single booking for itineraries that include the islands or culture circuit.

Are there any useful Bangla phrases to know?

A few that help along the way: ধন্যবাদ (dhonnobad) — thank you; কত? (koto) — how much?; ভালো (bhalo) — good. English is spoken to a reasonable degree in tourist-facing businesses in Cox's Bazar town.
