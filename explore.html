Cox's Bazar

Explore

The beach is the beginning. Nine distinct destinations stretch south from Cox's Bazar to the tip of Bangladesh.

Destinations

Where to go

Coral Island 9 km offshore

Saint Martin Island

Bangladesh's only coral island — 8 sq km of turquoise shallows, coral reefs, coconut groves, and the clearest water in the country. Reached by ship from Cox's Bazar only (Nov 1–Jan 31), under a daily visitor cap to protect the reef.

🐠 Snorkelling 🪸 Coral reefs 🌴 Overnight stays
Beach Zone 32 km south

Inani Beach

The most photographed stretch of Cox's Bazar coastline — embedded coral stones, natural rock formations, and teal water with far fewer visitors than the main beach.

📸 Photography 🪨 Coral stones 🌅 Sunrise
National Park 18 km south

Himchari

A waterfall tumbling through forested hills to the sea — most powerful in monsoon. The national park trail above offers the only elevated view of the full beach from the hills.

💧 Waterfall 🌿 Hill trail 🦋 Wildlife
Safari Park 50 km north

Dulahazara Safari Park

Bangladesh's largest safari park — 900 hectares of mixed forest with Asian elephants, deer, crocodiles, and over 200 bird species. A half-day trip from Cox's Bazar town.

🐘 Elephants 🦌 Safari trail 🦅 Birdwatching
Island 12 km by boat

Moheshkhali Island

A tidal island reached by motorboat — home to the ancient Adinath Shiva temple on a hilltop, salt flats, betel nut groves, and mangrove forests. A complete contrast to the beach.

🛕 Adinath Temple 🚤 Boat crossing 🌾 Salt flats
Peninsula 85 km south

Teknaf

The southern tip of the beach — where the Bay of Bengal meets the Naf River and Myanmar lies across the water, with the Teknaf Wildlife Reserve rising just inland.

🐦 Birdwatching 🏔️ Naf River views 🌿 Wildlife reserve
Eco Island 7 km offshore

Sonadia Island

An uninhabited tidal island of mangrove forest, mudflats, and sea grass beds — one of the most important bird habitats in Bangladesh. No hotels. No crowds. Pure wild coast.

🦅 Migratory birds 🌿 Mangroves 🔇 No crowds
Cultural Village Within town

Rakhain Village

The Rakhaine community's wooden stilt houses, hand-loom weaving workshops, and Buddhist Khyang temples are a short walk from the main beach — a thousand years of culture a five-minute ride away.

🧵 Weaving 🛕 Khyang temple 🎭 Festivals

Shopping

What to Bring Home

The Burmese Market in Tekpara is the obvious starting point — handwoven textiles, pickles, and goods found nowhere else in Bangladesh (see Culture & Heritage). Beyond that, look for Rakhine hand-loom weaving bought directly from village workshops, dried fish from Nazirartek for the adventurous, and simple beachwear and handicrafts along Laboni and Kolatoli. Bargaining is part of the experience in most markets and shops.

Travel With Care

Travelling Responsibly

Saint Martin's coral reef is now under active government protection — no coral or shell collection, no single-use plastics, a daily visitor cap, and a seasonal closure from February to October. The same care extends beyond the island: avoid buying coral, shell, or turtle-product souvenirs anywhere on the coast, pack out what you bring to the beach, and when visiting Rakhine villages, ask before photographing people and consider buying handwoven textiles directly from the weavers rather than mass-produced imports.
