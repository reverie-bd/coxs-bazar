Where to Stay

From beachfront suites to simple guesthouses — every budget, every style, all within reach of the shore.

Choose Your Area

Where you stay changes how you experience it

Kolatoli & Sugandha

The main hotel strip — most international-standard properties are here, within walking distance of the beach. Most convenient, most options, most activity.

Best for: First-timers, families, short stays

Laboni Beach Area

The older, more local end of the beach — busier, more chaotic, more authentic. Budget and mid-range guesthouses, beach-facing restaurants, real Cox's Bazar life.

Best for: Budget travellers, local experience

Inani & Marine Drive South

A handful of resort-style properties along the quieter southern stretch — more space, more privacy, dramatic coastal views, fewer crowds. The premium choice for longer stays.

Best for: Couples, longer stays, peace

By Budget

What to expect at every level

Luxury

BDT 8,000–25,000 / night

Beachfront resorts with sea-view rooms, pools, and full service. Long Beach Hotel, Seagull Hotel, and Ocean Paradise are the established names in this tier — international standard by Bangladeshi terms, well short of what that price buys in the region.

🏊 Pool🍽️ Restaurant🌊 Sea view🧖 Spa
Mid-Range

BDT 2,500–8,000 / night

Clean, comfortable hotels within a short walk of the beach — AC rooms, attached bathrooms, basic breakfast included at the better end. This is where most domestic tourists stay.

Budget

BDT 600–2,500 / night

Before You Book

Booking advice

📅

Book Early for Peak

November–February is high season. Popular hotels fill weeks in advance during Eid, public holidays, and winter weekends. Book at least 2–3 weeks ahead.

🌊

Ask for Sea View

Sea-view rooms are available at most beachfront hotels — simply request one when booking to guarantee an ocean-facing room.

🏷️

Negotiate Off-Season

During monsoon (June–September) rates drop dramatically — 40–60% off peak prices are common. The beach is rough but the hills are at their greenest.

📍

Stay Central First

If it's your first visit, stay near Kolatoli or Sugandha. You can always move further out for a second trip once you understand the geography.
