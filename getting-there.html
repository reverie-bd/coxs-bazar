Getting There

From anywhere in the world to the longest beach in Asia — here's the route.

Arriving From Abroad

Getting Here, Wherever You're Coming From

International visitors arrive through Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport in Dhaka, well connected to major hubs across the Middle East, South and Southeast Asia, and Europe. From there, reaching Cox's Bazar is part of the everyday rhythm of travel here — a quick domestic flight, a scenic train ride, or an overnight bus, each with its own character.

Getting There

From Dhaka to Cox's Bazar

By Air

The fastest option — Dhaka (DAC) to Cox's Bazar (CXB) takes just 1 hour, with Biman Bangladesh, US-Bangla, and Novoair operating multiple flights daily.

⏱ 1 hour💰 BDT 3,500–8,000

By Bus

Direct AC buses from Dhaka's Sayedabad terminal — depart evening, arrive morning. Shyamoli, Hanif, and S Alam are the main operators. Comfortable enough for an overnight journey.

⏱ 10–12 hours💰 BDT 700–1,400

By Train + Bus

Train from Dhaka to Chittagong (7 hrs), then bus or shared car from Chittagong to Cox's Bazar (3 hrs). Longer but scenic — the train through the countryside is worth doing once.

⏱ 10–11 hours💰 BDT 500–1,200

Getting Around

Local transport

CNG Auto-Rickshaw

The main way to move around — negotiate a price before you get in. Marine Drive end-to-end takes about 3 hours and costs BDT 800–1,200 negotiated.

Speed Boat

For Moheshkhali and Sonadia — speed boats from the main jetty. Shared boats run on demand; private hire is negotiable. Always agree price before boarding.

Ship to Saint Martin's

Tourist ships depart from Cox's Bazar — the Teknaf and Inani routes are currently closed. The season runs November 1 – January 31, with a daily visitor cap and advance booking required.

Motorbike Hire

Available for hire near Kolatoli — BDT 400–600 per day. The most flexible way to explore Marine Drive at your own pace. International licence recommended.
