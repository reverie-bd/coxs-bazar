Did You Know?

A few surprising facts about the coast, for the curious.

Trivia

Ten things worth knowing

🏖️

Third-Longest Beach

Cox's Bazar is the third-longest beach in the world, behind Brazil's Praia do Cassino and Australia's Eighty Mile Beach.

🏆

New7Wonders Finalist

Between 2008 and 2011, it was named an official finalist in the New7Wonders of Nature campaign, alongside Komodo Island and the Amazon Rainforest.

⚓

Named After a Captain

The name comes from Captain Hiram Cox, a British East India Company officer posted here in 1798.

🐟

Dried Fish Capital

Nazirartek, just north of the main beach, produces around 85% of Bangladesh's entire dried fish supply.

🦒

Bangladesh's Only Coral Island

Saint Martin Island is the only coral island in Bangladesh.

🏛️

Over 1,200 Years of History

The Rakhine community has called this coast home since the 9th century — over 1,200 years of continuous presence.

🛣️

80 km of Coastal Road

Marine Drive, the coastal road running the length of the beach, stretches 80 km — about 3 hours end to end.

📅

A Different Weekend

Bangladesh's weekend falls on Friday and Saturday, not Saturday and Sunday — useful to know when planning around bank or market hours.

🐘

Bangladesh's Largest Safari Park

Nearby Dulahazara Safari Park is Bangladesh's largest, spanning roughly 900 hectares.

🌼

Once Called Panowa

Long before "Cox's Bazar," this coast was known as Panowa — meaning "yellow flower."
