Cox's Bazar

Activities

From surfing at dawn to diving coral reefs — the coast offers more than most people expect.

On the Water

Sea & Shore

Surfing

Cox's Bazar has a growing surf scene — the waves are best November to March. Boards available for hire at Laboni and Kolatoli beach areas.

⭐ Best: Nov–Mar📍 Laboni Beach

Parasailing

Rise above the beach and see the full length of the coast from the air — the 120 km curve of shore visible from altitude is unforgettable.

⭐ Best: Nov–Apr📍 Kolatoli

Scuba Diving & Snorkelling

Saint Martin's Island is the only place in Bangladesh with coral reefs significant enough for diving. Operators run day trips from Cox's Bazar during peak season.

⭐ Best: Nov–Mar📍 Saint Martin's

Boat Tours

Sunset boat rides from the main jetty, island-hopping to Sonadia and Moheshkhali, and the full-day ship journey to Saint Martin's — boats are central to life here.

⭐ Year-round📍 Main Jetty

Horse Riding

One of Cox's Bazar's most iconic images — horses cantering along the wet sand at low tide. Handlers with horses are available at Laboni Beach throughout the day.

⭐ Year-round📍 Laboni Beach

Deep Sea Fishing

Charter a trawler for a full-day deep sea fishing trip — the Bay of Bengal yields tuna, red snapper, and barracuda. A completely different way to experience the coast.

⭐ Best: Nov–Apr📍 Fishing Harbour

On Land

Beyond the shoreline

Hill Trails

Himchari National Park has forest trails rising above the beach — the only place you can look down on the full coastline. Best in the green post-monsoon season.

Golf

Cox's Bazar Golf Club — a nine-hole course near the town centre, one of the few coastal golf venues in Bangladesh. Equipment available for hire.

Sea Aquarium

Radiant Fish World — Bangladesh's first sea aquarium, with live coral displays, ray tanks, and marine species from the Bay of Bengal. A good option with children.

Birdwatching

Sonadia Island and the Teknaf Wildlife Reserve are internationally significant birdwatching sites — migratory shorebirds arrive October to March in large numbers.
